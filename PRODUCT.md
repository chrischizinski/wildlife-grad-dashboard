# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students, advisors, graduate-program staff, and natural resources faculty use this dashboard to understand the U.S. wildlife and natural resources graduate assistantship market. They are usually scanning for evidence: posting volume, discipline mix, compensation transparency, geography, and data quality.

## Product Purpose

The product turns scraped assistantship postings into a public, static, evidence-oriented dashboard. It should help users answer practical market questions quickly while keeping uncertainty and data-quality limits visible.

## Brand Personality

Calm, credible, field-aware. The interface should feel like a careful analytical tool, not a marketing site or a decorative data poster.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard tropes: glossy hero-metric layouts, shiny stacked cards, decorative gradients, gratuitous motion, and repeated icon-card grids. Avoid overstating precision when the underlying data is parsed or classified.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before ornament: charts, labels, filters, and data quality indicators should carry the interface.
- Trust through constraints: show denominators, parsing coverage, timestamps, and suppression thresholds clearly.
- Dense but readable: make repeated scanning efficient without burying new users.
- Familiar controls: use standard navigation, filters, and chart controls with consistent affordances.
- Publicly accountable: every visible metric should make its data source or limitation understandable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve keyboard-operable controls, visible focus states, sufficient text/background contrast, reduced-motion friendly transitions, and chart alternatives through labels, legends, and empty states.
